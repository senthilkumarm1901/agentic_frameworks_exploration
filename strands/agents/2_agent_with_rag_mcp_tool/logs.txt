Question: Describe the political system of Germany.
Answer:
Germany operates as a **federal parliamentary republic**. In this system:

- The **Federal President** (Bundespräsident) serves as the head of state, with largely ceremonial duties.
- The **Chancellor** (Bundeskanzler) is the head of government and holds the primary executive power.
- The legislative branch consists of two houses:
  - The **Bundestag** (Federal Diet), directly elected by the people.
  - The **Bundesrat** (Federal Council), representing the 16 federal states (Länder).
- Germany follows a system of **federalism**, where powers are divided between the national government and the states.
- The country has a multi-party system, with the **Basic Law** (Grundgesetz) serving as its constitution.
