Question: Describe the political system of Germany.
Answer:
Germany operates as a **federal parliamentary republic**. In this system:

- The **Federal President** (Bundespräsident) serves as the head of state, primarily performing ceremonial duties.
- The **Chancellor** (Bundeskanzler) is the head of government and holds executive power, responsible for running the government and setting policy direction.
- The **Bundestag** (Federal Diet) is the primary legislative body, elected by the people, and chooses the Chancellor.
- The **Bundesrat** (Federal Council) represents the 16 federal states (Länder) and participates in legislation, especially on matters affecting state interests.
- Germany follows a **multi-party system**, and the Chancellor is typically the leader of the party or coalition that wins the majority in the Bundestag.
- The **Federal Constitutional Court** ensures that all government actions comply with the Basic Law (Grundgesetz), Germany's constitution.

This structure emphasizes the rule of law, democratic representation, and a balance of power between federal and state authorities.
