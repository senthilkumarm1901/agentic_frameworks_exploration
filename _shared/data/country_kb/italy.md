# Italy

- Italy has more UNESCO World Heritage Sites than any other country in the world.
- The Roman Empire, centered in Italy, shaped Western civilization's legal systems, architecture, and governance structures.
- Italy is the birthplace of the Renaissance, which originated in Florence during the 14th century.
- Vatican City, an independent city-state within Rome, is the smallest country in the world and the spiritual center of Catholicism.
- Italian cuisine varies significantly by region, with distinct traditions in pasta, cheese, and wine production across its 20 regions.
- Italy has the oldest population in Europe, with one of the highest median ages of any country globally.
- The country is home to over half of Europe's art treasures, housed in museums, churches, and archaeological sites.
- Italian is a direct descendant of Latin and is the closest modern language to the language of ancient Rome.
