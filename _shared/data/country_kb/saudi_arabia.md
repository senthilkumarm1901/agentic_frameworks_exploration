# Saudi Arabia

- Saudi Arabia is the birthplace of Islam and home to the two holiest cities in the religion: Mecca and Medina.
- The country operates as an absolute monarchy, with the King serving as both head of state and head of government.
- Saudi Arabia has no permanent rivers, making it one of the largest countries in the world without any natural lakes or rivers.
- The Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca is one of the largest annual gatherings of people in the world, with millions of Muslims participating each year.
- Women were granted the right to drive in Saudi Arabia in 2018, following decades of activism.
- The Empty Quarter (Rub' al Khali) in Saudi Arabia is the largest continuous sand desert in the world.
- Saudi Arabia is the world's largest exporter of petroleum and holds some of the largest proven oil reserves globally.
- The country follows Sharia law, with Islamic principles forming the basis of its legal and judicial system.
