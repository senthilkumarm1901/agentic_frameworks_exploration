# Egypt

- Egypt is home to the only surviving wonder of the ancient world, the Great Pyramid of Giza, built over 4,500 years ago.
- The Nile River, flowing through Egypt, is the longest river in the world and the source of Egypt's agricultural civilization.
- Ancient Egypt developed one of the earliest writing systems, hieroglyphics, which remained undeciphered until the Rosetta Stone's discovery in 1799.
- The Suez Canal, opened in 1869, connects the Mediterranean Sea to the Red Sea and is one of the world's most heavily used shipping lanes.
- Egypt is considered the cradle of civilization, with continuous habitation along the Nile for over 5,000 years.
- Al-Azhar University in Cairo, founded in 970 CE, is one of the oldest continuously operating universities in the world.
- Egypt spans two continents, with the Sinai Peninsula located in Asia while the rest of the country is in Africa.
- The ancient Egyptians were among the first to develop a 365-day calendar, which became the basis for the modern Gregorian calendar.
