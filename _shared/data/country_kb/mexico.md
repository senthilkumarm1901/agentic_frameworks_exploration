# Mexico

- Mexico has the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Americas and is home to ancient Mayan and Aztec civilizations.
- The country is the birthplace of chocolate, with the Aztecs and Mayans cultivating cacao for thousands of years.
- Mexican cuisine was inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2010, recognizing its ancient traditions.
- Mexico City was built on the ruins of Tenochtitlan, the capital of the Aztec Empire, which was founded on an island in Lake Texcoco.
- The country has the most Spanish speakers of any nation in the world, surpassing Spain itself.
- Mexico is one of the world's most biodiverse countries, ranking among the top five for species of plants, reptiles, and mammals.
- The Day of the Dead (Día de los Muertos) is a unique Mexican tradition blending indigenous Aztec rituals with Catholic practices.
- Mexico produces more than half of the world's supply of avocados, with the state of Michoacán as the primary growing region.
