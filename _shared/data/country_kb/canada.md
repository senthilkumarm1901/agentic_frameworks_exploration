# Canada

- Canada has the longest coastline of any country in the world, bordered by three oceans: the Atlantic, Pacific, and Arctic.
- The country is officially bilingual at the federal level, with English and French as its two official languages.
- Canada contains approximately 20% of the world's freshwater, including the Great Lakes shared with the United States.
- The Canadian Shield, one of the oldest geological formations on Earth, covers nearly half of Canada's landmass.
- Canada operates as a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy, with the British monarch as head of state.
- The country has the longest undefended border in the world, sharing it with the United States.
- Canada was the first country to adopt an official multiculturalism policy in 1971, enshrining cultural diversity in law.
- The Trans-Canada Highway is one of the longest national highways in the world, stretching from the Atlantic to the Pacific coast.
