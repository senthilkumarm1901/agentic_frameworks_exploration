# Australia

- Australia is the only country that is also a continent, making it the world's largest island and smallest continent.
- The Great Barrier Reef off Australia's northeast coast is the world's largest coral reef system, visible from space.
- Australia has more species of venomous snakes than any other continent, including the inland taipan, the world's most venomous snake.
- The country operates as a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy, with the British monarch as head of state.
- Indigenous Australians have the oldest continuous culture on Earth, with archaeological evidence dating back at least 65,000 years.
- Australia is the driest inhabited continent, with approximately 70% of its landmass classified as arid or semi-arid.
- The Australian Outback covers most of the continent's interior and is one of the largest remote wilderness areas in the world.
- Australia introduced compulsory voting in 1924, and citizens can be fined for failing to vote in federal elections.
