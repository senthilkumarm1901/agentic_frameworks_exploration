# United Kingdom

- The United Kingdom comprises four constituent countries: England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.
- The UK operates as a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy, with the monarch serving as head of state.
- The British Empire was the largest empire in history, at its height controlling approximately one-quarter of the world's land area.
- The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the late 18th century, transforming manufacturing and spreading globally.
- English common law, developed in England, has influenced legal systems across North America, Australia, and many former British colonies.
- The BBC (British Broadcasting Corporation) is the world's oldest national broadcasting organization, founded in 1922.
- The UK has produced more Nobel Prize winners in literature than any country except France, including authors like William Shakespeare and Charles Dickens.
- The London Underground, opened in 1863, is the world's oldest underground railway system.
