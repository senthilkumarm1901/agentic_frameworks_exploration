# Biodiversity and Rainforests

- Australia: The Great Barrier Reef off Australia's northeast coast is the world's largest coral reef system, visible from space.
- Australia: Australia has more species of venomous snakes than any other continent, including the inland taipan, the world's most venomous snake.
- Brazil: Brazil contains approximately 60% of the Amazon rainforest, the world's largest tropical forest and a critical carbon sink.
- Brazil: The Amazon River, which flows through Brazil, carries more water than any other river system on Earth.
- Brazil: Brazil has the most biodiversity of any country, with more species of plants, freshwater fish, and mammals than any other nation.
- Indonesia: The Indonesian island of Borneo (shared with Malaysia and Brunei) is home to some of the oldest rainforests on Earth.
- Indonesia: Indonesia contains the world's third-largest tropical rainforest after the Amazon and Congo Basin.
- Indonesia: The country is home to the Komodo dragon, the world's largest living lizard species, found only on a few Indonesian islands.
- Mexico: Mexico is one of the world's most biodiverse countries, ranking among the top five for species of plants, reptiles, and mammals.
