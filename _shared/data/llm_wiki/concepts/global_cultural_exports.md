# Global Cultural Exports

- Brazil: Carnival in Rio de Janeiro is the world's largest festival, attracting over 2 million people per day during the celebrations.
- France: French cuisine was inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2010.
- Germany: Germany has produced more classical music composers than any other country, including Bach, Beethoven, Brahms, and Wagner.
- Italy: Italy has more UNESCO World Heritage Sites than any other country in the world.
- Italy: Italian cuisine varies significantly by region, with distinct traditions in pasta, cheese, and wine production across its 20 regions.
- Japan: Japanese cuisine (washoku) was designated a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2013.
- Mexico: Mexico has the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Americas and is home to ancient Mayan and Aztec civilizations.
- Mexico: Mexican cuisine was inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2010, recognizing its ancient traditions.
- Nigeria: Nollywood, Nigeria's film industry, is the second-largest by volume of films produced annually, behind only India's Bollywood.
- South Africa: South Africa is home to the Cradle of Humankind, a UNESCO site with the richest concentration of human ancestral fossils in the world.
- South Korea: K-pop (Korean pop music) has become a global cultural phenomenon, with groups like BTS achieving worldwide commercial success.
- South Korea: South Korean cinema gained global recognition when "Parasite" became the first non-English language film to win the Academy Award for Best Picture in 2020.
- Spain: Spain has the second-largest number of UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the world, after Italy.
- Spain: Spain's La Tomatina festival in Buñol is the world's largest tomato fight, held annually on the last Wednesday of August.
- Spain: The Running of the Bulls (Encierro) in Pamplona is one of the most famous and controversial traditional festivals in the world.
- United States: Hollywood, based in Los Angeles, produces the majority of the world's highest-grossing films.
