# Aging and Demographics

- Brazil: It is the only Portuguese-speaking country in the Americas and the largest in South America by both area and population.
- Canada: The country is officially bilingual at the federal level, with English and French as its two official languages.
- Canada: Canada was the first country to adopt an official multiculturalism policy in 1971, enshrining cultural diversity in law.
- China: Mandarin Chinese is the most spoken native language in the world, with multiple regional dialects across the country.
- France: The Académie française, founded in 1635, is the official authority on the French language and its usage.
- Germany: German is the most widely spoken native language in the European Union.
- India: India is the world's largest democracy by population, with a parliamentary system of government.
- India: The country has 22 officially recognized languages and over 19,500 dialects spoken across its states.
- Indonesia: The country has the largest Muslim population of any nation, though it maintains a secular constitution with religious pluralism.
- Indonesia: Bahasa Indonesia, the national language, was created as a unifying lingua franca for the country's diverse ethnic groups.
- Italy: Italy has the oldest population in Europe, with one of the highest median ages of any country globally.
- Italy: Italian is a direct descendant of Latin and is the closest modern language to the language of ancient Rome.
- Japan: It has the oldest population in the world, with over 29% of citizens aged 65 or older.
- Japan: Japan's literacy rate is nearly 99%, one of the highest in the world.
- Nigeria: The country has a young population, with a median age of approximately 18 years.
- Russia: The Russian language uses the Cyrillic alphabet, which was developed in the First Bulgarian Empire during the 9th century.
- South Africa: The country has eleven official languages, the most of any nation, reflecting its diverse ethnic composition.
- South Korea: South Korean cinema gained global recognition when "Parasite" became the first non-English language film to win the Academy Award for Best Picture in 2020.
- Spain: The Spanish language originated in the Castile region and is now the world's fourth most spoken language by total speakers.
