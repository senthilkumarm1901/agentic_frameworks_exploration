# Constitutional Monarchies

- Australia: Australia is the only country that is also a continent, making it the world's largest island and smallest continent.
- Australia: The country operates as a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy, with the British monarch as head of state.
- Brazil: It is the only Portuguese-speaking country in the Americas and the largest in South America by both area and population.
- Canada: Canada operates as a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy, with the British monarch as head of state.
- China: The Terracotta Army in Xi'an, discovered in 1974, contains over 8,000 life-sized clay soldiers guarding Emperor Qin Shi Huang's tomb.
- Japan: The country has a constitutional monarchy with the Emperor serving as a ceremonial figurehead; the Prime Minister holds executive power.
- Mexico: Mexico is one of the world's most biodiverse countries, ranking among the top five for species of plants, reptiles, and mammals.
- Saudi Arabia: The country operates as an absolute monarchy, with the King serving as both head of state and head of government.
- Saudi Arabia: Saudi Arabia has no permanent rivers, making it one of the largest countries in the world without any natural lakes or rivers.
- South Korea: The Korean alphabet, Hangul, was invented in the 15th century by King Sejong the Great and is considered one of the most scientific writing systems.
- Spain: Spain operates as a parliamentary constitutional monarchy, with the King as head of state and a Prime Minister leading the government.
- United Kingdom: The United Kingdom comprises four constituent countries: England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.
- United Kingdom: The UK operates as a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy, with the monarch serving as head of state.
- United States: The US interstate highway system spans over 48,000 miles, making it one of the largest road networks in the world.
