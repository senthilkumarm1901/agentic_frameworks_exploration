# Ancient Civilizations

- China: The Great Wall of China is the longest human-made structure ever built, stretching over 13,000 miles across northern China.
- China: China has the world's oldest continuous civilization, with written records dating back over 3,500 years.
- China: China invented paper, printing, gunpowder, and the compass, known as the Four Great Inventions of ancient China.
- Egypt: Egypt is home to the only surviving wonder of the ancient world, the Great Pyramid of Giza, built over 4,500 years ago.
- Egypt: The Nile River, flowing through Egypt, is the longest river in the world and the source of Egypt's agricultural civilization.
- Egypt: Ancient Egypt developed one of the earliest writing systems, hieroglyphics, which remained undeciphered until the Rosetta Stone's discovery in 1799.
- Egypt: Egypt is considered the cradle of civilization, with continuous habitation along the Nile for over 5,000 years.
- Egypt: The ancient Egyptians were among the first to develop a 365-day calendar, which became the basis for the modern Gregorian calendar.
- Italy: The Roman Empire, centered in Italy, shaped Western civilization's legal systems, architecture, and governance structures.
- Italy: Italian is a direct descendant of Latin and is the closest modern language to the language of ancient Rome.
- Mexico: Mexico has the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Americas and is home to ancient Mayan and Aztec civilizations.
- Mexico: The country is the birthplace of chocolate, with the Aztecs and Mayans cultivating cacao for thousands of years.
- Mexico: Mexican cuisine was inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2010, recognizing its ancient traditions.
- Mexico: Mexico City was built on the ruins of Tenochtitlan, the capital of the Aztec Empire, which was founded on an island in Lake Texcoco.
- Mexico: The Day of the Dead (Día de los Muertos) is a unique Mexican tradition blending indigenous Aztec rituals with Catholic practices.
- Russia: The Russian language uses the Cyrillic alphabet, which was developed in the First Bulgarian Empire during the 9th century.
- United Kingdom: The British Empire was the largest empire in history, at its height controlling approximately one-quarter of the world's land area.
