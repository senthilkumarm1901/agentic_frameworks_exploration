# Indonesia - Geography and Environment

- Indonesia is the world's largest archipelago nation, comprising over 17,000 islands stretching across the equator.
- Indonesia's Krakatoa volcano produced one of the most catastrophic eruptions in recorded history in 1883.
- The Indonesian island of Borneo (shared with Malaysia and Brunei) is home to some of the oldest rainforests on Earth.
- Indonesia contains the world's third-largest tropical rainforest after the Amazon and Congo Basin.
- The country is home to the Komodo dragon, the world's largest living lizard species, found only on a few Indonesian islands.
- Indonesia sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire and has more active volcanoes than any other country in the world.
